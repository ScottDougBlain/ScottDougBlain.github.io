Seeing Yourself Seen: Gaze Perception and the Architecture of Social Awareness

Accurately perceiving when one is the focus of someone else's attention is foundational to social cognition. This ability enables empathy and communication, but also creates vulnerability to misinterpretation and manipulation—in both humans and AI systems.

Human Social Cognition

Social cognitive abilities include processes that allow us to understand the minds of others—including emotion perception and theory of mind. My research has shown that social cognitive abilities exhibit a dual-use nature. Many of the capabilities underpinning compassion also enable manipulative deception. As AI systems develop increasingly complex social intelligence, we must ensure these abilities are channeled for prosocial means.

Emotion perception and facial recognition form the foundation of human social intelligence—capabilities that enable both empathy and manipulation.

From Human Theory of Mind to AI Alignment

Just as humans with enhanced theory of mind can choose cooperation or manipulation, AI systems with ToM capabilities face the same crossroads. This is not a theoretical concern—it's an existential challenge.

An AI that can model human beliefs at multiple levels could predict and manipulate human behavior with unprecedented sophistication. My research on human social cognition provides crucial insights for navigating this challenge.

Implications for Future AI: From Human Biases to AI Biases
Self-Referential Processing in AI

Understanding human self-referential biases is crucial for AI alignment. An AI with too strong a self-referential bias might misinterpret neutral interactions as being "about" it, leading to paranoid or defensive behaviors.

Evidence Accumulation Efficiency

An AI with inefficient evidence accumulation (like in schizophrenia) might require excessive certainty before acting, leading to paralysis. Conversely, too-efficient accumulation could lead to premature conclusions.

The Cooperation-Deception Spectrum

The same mechanisms enabling AI to understand human intentions could enable manipulation. How do we build AI systems that can understand social cues without exploiting them?
