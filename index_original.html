Neural Network Findings

Brain Network Analysis: Default (orange) and Frontoparietal (green) Networks

Social Intelligence & AI Alignment

The Double-Edged Sword of Social Intelligence

My research reveals a fundamental paradox in social cognition: the same theory of mind abilities that enable compassion and cooperation also enable manipulation and deception. This dual-use nature has profound implications for AI alignment.

In studies with 335+ participants, I found that while compassion predicted better theory of mind, so did manipulativeness‚Äîhighlighting how enhanced social reasoning serves both prosocial and antisocial ends.

The Science: Neural and Behavioral Evidence

Neural Mechanisms

Default network activation during theory of mind tasks predicts both social cognitive ability AND agreeableness (N=1050). Key regions include the dorsal medial prefrontal cortex and temporoparietal junction‚Äîthe same networks that could enable AI deception if not properly aligned.

Empirical Evidence

My research identified a three-factor structure of social behavior: Compassion-Callousness, Pacifism-Aggression, and Honesty-Manipulativeness. Critically, Machiavellianism was associated with better social cognition, demonstrating how enhanced mentalizing abilities can serve antisocial ends.
